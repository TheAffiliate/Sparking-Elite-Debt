Definitions

Credit Bureau:

A company that collects the credit behaviour of individuals such as their payment history, adverse information (judgements/Defaults) and makes them available to companies that off their goods or services on credited ITC (Trans union – Trust Corporation,XDS or Experia.

Payment History:

A 24 month track record on how an individual pays his /her accounts

Judgement:

An Adverse report on an individual placed by their credit provider through a court order then to the credit Bureau on how negative they have conducted their account. This is usually kept on their record for 5 years.

Debt Review:

(i.e. Debt Counselling) Is a firm of debt rehabilitation whereby a consumer settles his debt obligations to creditors with the assistance of his Debt Counsellor and usually a payment agency. The Debt Counsellor re- structures the debt of the consumer by extending the contractual repayment period so that the consumer is able to afford the restructured instalment and able to maintain himself and his family. The consumer pays one monthly instalment to a registered payment agency and his amount is then distributed to the creditors by the payment agency, this usually kept on their record for 10 years.

Administration:

Is similar to debt review, the main difference is that you approach a lawyer od debt administrator to restructure your debt with your creditors, this usually kept on their record for 10 years.

Sequestration:

Sequestration is a legal process by which you are declared insolvent by an order of the high court of South Africa. The effect of sequestration order is that your debts (In layman’s terms) are I written off. In legal Terms, your debt vests on a Trustee, that needs to sell your assets and distribute the proceeds according to the insolvency Act, this usually kept on their records for 15 years

Garnishee Order:

The most common type of garnishment is wage garnishment which is the process of deduction money from an employee’s monetary compensation (including salary), sometimes as a result of a court order. Wage garnishment continue until the entire debt is paid or arrangements are made to pay of the debt
